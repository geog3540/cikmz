Flow Mapping Chicago Bike Trips in 2021

The flow map demonstrates bike trips in Chicago in April 2021. Point symbols represent bike stations and were classified based on the gross volume of flows, which means the total of pick-ups and drop-offs. On the other hand, flows show where bike trips are from station to station. When looking at the spatial pattern of the flow map, it is noticeable that most bicycle trips are in the south and north direction, according to the flow directions. At the same time, considering the numerical values of the flows, it seems that the highest number of trips occurs in coastal areas. It can be interpreted that the reason for this is that bicycle users go on trips not only for entertainment or transportation purposes but also for tourism purposes. Considering the numerical values of the points, gross flow decreases as you move away from downtown Chicago. However, considering only the bicycle stations in the south and north, it is interesting that the stations in the south are used less than the stations in the north. The main reason for this is Chicago's distinct population (including race and median household income) distribution fact.
